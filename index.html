Post-Growth Toolbox

To live well within planetary boundaries we need to fundamentally reconsider how we live, and the practicalities of how to actually change our ways.

To achieve such a society does not mean doing less of the same, but instead that everything will need to be different:

"...different activities, different forms and uses of energy, different relations, different gender roles, different allocations of time between paid and non-paid work, different relations with the non-human world" (Kallis et al., 2014, p.33)

Below you find compartments of a post-growth toolbox - a gathering of ways to do things differently.

DICTIONARY

Planetary Boundaries

A concept presenting a set of nine Earth system processes within which humanity can continue to develop and thrive for generations to come. Crossing these boundaries increases the risk of generating large-scale abrupt or irreversible environmental changes.

Ref: Kallis et al., 2025.

Degrowth

A political, economic, and social movement based on ecological economics, anti-consumerism, and anti-capitalism. It advocates for the equitable downscaling of production and consumption that increases human well-being and enhances ecological conditions at the local and global level.

Ref: D'Alisa, Demaria, & Kallis, 2014.

Limits to Growth

The concept that exponential economic and population growth cannot continue indefinitely on a planet with finite resources. Initially modeled and popularized in a 1972 report commissioned by the Club of Rome.

Ref: Meadows et al., 1972.
